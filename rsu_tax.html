Limitations and Assumptions

Right now, I'm only able to calculate tax estimates as if you earned restricted stock units (RSUs) in San Francisco, California. I'm calculating California tax estimates based on the 2018 brackets, since 2019 hasn't been released yet. I can't handle stock options. I can't handle tax filing statuses of Married Filing Separately or Qualifying Widow(er). Also, I don't think about or process complex income like rents, royalties, retirement/social security, long-term capital gains/losses, or self-employment. I assume cash bonuses and RSU grants are withheld separately at supplemental tax rates (i.e. not part of your normal paycheck) and that cash bonuses are paid at the beginning of the year, subject to full FICA withholding. I don't factor in credits or deductions beyond standard/itemized deductions (e.g. earned income credit, child tax credit, student loan interest deduction, education credits, and dependent care expenses). I assume all of your base income is subject to normal withholding that is equivalent to your actual taxes owed on that income. I assume your RSUs vest monthly on the first each month and that your paychecks come twice per month on the 1st and 15th. Finally, if you're Married Filing Jointly or have dependents, I assume everyone is earning income with a uniform base income and RSU structure, subject to standard withholding.
